Part 1

Voucher Redemption

Redemption is the process of exchanging points for a voucher. This section covers what information the member provides during redemption — specifically the "for me or others" choice and the additional info form per voucher category.

1.1 For Me or For Others

At redemption, the member selects whether the voucher is for themselves or for someone else. This choice is locked at redemption and cannot be changed after. Switching from "for others" back to self (or vice versa) after redemption would mean the redemption email already sent to the recipient becomes invalid — creating confusion for the recipient and requiring email revocation logic. Locking it avoids this complexity.

Field Rule
Self / Others Mandatory. Locked once submitted — cannot be switched after redemption.
Receiver name Mandatory if "for others." Locked once submitted.
Receiver email Optional. Can only be filled at redemption — no fill-later. If provided, triggers a redemption email with the voucher to the recipient. Locked once submitted.
i
Business & Travel – Points Exchange: No separate self/others step. The member enters the external account ID to receive the points (e.g. Krisflyer member number). This could be their own account or someone else's — the flow is the same either way, since the member just inputs the target account ID in the form field.
*
Bulk gifting edge case: One or two high-volume members redeem many gift vouchers at once for different recipients (e.g. 10 restaurant gift vouchers for 10 different employees). The current flow requires "for others" to be selected per voucher at redemption, so a multi-quantity redemption assigns the same recipient to all vouchers. Current workaround: the member redeems separately with quantity 1 per recipient. Future consideration: if demand grows, an admin reassignment feature on the Ops Dashboard could allow the Rewards team to take a batch of vouchers redeemed by a single member and reassign them to different recipients on their behalf — essentially a concierge service where the member provides a list (e.g. via email or spreadsheet) and the admin team handles the individual assignments, generating the appropriate redemption emails for each recipient. This keeps the self-serve flow simple for all users while giving power users a supported path.

1.2 Additional Info

Some voucher categories collect additional information at redemption. All categories have a Notes field with dynamic placeholder text tailored to the category — giving contextual hints about what's useful to include (e.g. preferred dates for hotel vouchers, dietary preferences for dining) without making any field mandatory. This is a frontend-only concern: the form structure and validation stay the same, only the placeholder string changes per category.

On-site utilization vouchers (Dining, Wellness & Rec, Gift – restaurant, Retail collection, B&T – Others)

These vouchers are used as payment method at the outlet. Additional info is optional and available at redemption only — no fill-later flow. If the member needs to communicate something after redemption, they can email support.

These 5 vouchers could be used as 5 nights in 1 room, 5 rooms for 1 night, or split across different stays. Capturing room count at redemption doesn't work because the allocation depends on how the member decides to use them later. This is better handled during the booking/fulfillment process where the reservations team is involved.

1.3 Post-Redemption CTA

After redemption, the voucher detail page shows a call-to-action that opens a prefilled email to the Rewards team. This appears for both the member (in their redeemed voucher list) and the recipient (on the voucher page accessed via the redemption email link). The CTA gives users a direct path to coordinate with the team without building a fill-later form.

Hotel / Gift voucher – hotel
CTA label: "Ready to book? Contact us"
These categories need reservation coordination (dates, rooms, special requests). The CTA opens a prefilled email to the reservations/Rewards inbox.
Dining / Wellness / Gift – restaurant / Retail / B&T – Others
CTA label: "Questions about this voucher? Contact us"
Lighter wording — these are typically walk-in/on-site vouchers. The CTA serves as a general support channel.
Email field Content
To Dedicated Rewards inbox (e.g. rewards@ayana.com)
Subject Voucher Inquiry — [Voucher Code] [Voucher Name]
Body Prefilled with: member name, membership tier, voucher type, quantity, voucher code. For "for others" recipients, the body identifies them as the recipient and includes the gifter's name.
i
Who sees it: For "for me" vouchers, the member sees the CTA on their voucher detail page. For "for others" vouchers, both the member (in their redeemed voucher list) and the recipient (on the voucher page from the redemption email) see the CTA — with slightly different prefilled email body text so the Rewards team knows who they're speaking with.
*
Exception — B&T – Points Exchange: No CTA. Points Exchange vouchers are processed automatically once the account details are submitted at redemption. There is nothing to coordinate after.

Part 2

Voucher Utilization

Utilization is the process of using a redeemed voucher at an outlet or having it fulfilled by the Rewards admin. This part covers the current state, the initial proposed flow, the revised proposal, fraud prevention, and open items.

Section 2.1

How Voucher Utilization Works Today

Today, voucher utilization runs on a simple manual process: the member shows their voucher code (on their phone or screen), and staff searches for it in the Rewards system to mark it as used. There is no QR scanning, no Field App integration, and no guest app involved in the utilization flow.

This works, but it has known limitations: the voucher code is exposed in plain text (fraud risk), there's no built-in identity verification (anyone who knows the code could use it), and it relies on staff manually looking up codes in a web dashboard.

Section 2.2

Before implementation, a detailed utilization flow was designed to accommodate different voucher categories, outlet capabilities, and user types. The full matrix is in the Appendix. This section summarizes the key design decisions.

Initial Utilization Methods

For on-site vouchers, the initial design defined 5 different utilization methods based on three branching factors:

Outlet capability
Does the property have a Field App (and guest app)?
User type
Is the voucher being used by the member (self) or someone else (others)?
Guest status
Is the user a staying guest or a non-staying guest / external visitor?

These three factors produce the following methods:

# Method When used Initiated by
1 Staff via Field App (no QR scan) Property has Field App, voucher for self Staff
2 Member & staff scan QR on Rewards website Property has Field App, voucher for self Guest + Staff
3 Member & staff scan QR on redemption email Property has Field App, voucher for others Guest + Staff
4 Member scans outlet QR via Rewards website No Field App, voucher for self Guest
5 Guest scans outlet QR via Utilization Landing Page No Field App, voucher for others Guest

Assessment

Before building this, we revisited the design to see if it could be improved. The assessment focuses on two specific areas where the initial flow could be better:

Guest-initiated multi-step flows (methods 4–5)
In methods 4 and 5, the guest must drive the entire process: scan an outlet QR code, navigate to a website or landing page, find and select the correct voucher, and show confirmation to staff. This is a multi-step guest-initiated flow that could be replaced with a simpler staff-initiated approach. (Note: methods 2–3, where the guest shows a QR and staff scans it, are lighter and remain in the revised proposal.)
Staying guest experience (method 1) — needs clarification
Method 1 ("staff via Field App, no QR") is underspecified — it doesn't define how staff identifies which guest's voucher to use. The revised proposal makes this clearer by specifying a room number or guest name lookup via PMS integration in the Field App. The method itself is the same; we're just documenting the steps explicitly.
*
What stays the same: The branching factors (outlet capability, user type, guest status) still apply. Guests still need to show a QR on their phone for method 2. Self vs others still determines where the QR comes from (account vs email). Staff still needs to know the situation. The category-to-nature mapping and admin-fulfilled flow are unchanged.
Section 2.3

Revised Proposed Utilization Flow

The revised proposal makes one targeted change to the initial design (replacing guest-initiated outlet QR flows with a staff web tool), clarifies one underspecified method (room lookup for staying guests), and keeps the rest intact.

Voucher Nature Classification (Unchanged)

The two-nature classification from the initial design remains the same. Admin-fulfilled vouchers are handled via Ops Dashboard; on-site vouchers require staff action at the outlet. The voucher category determines when utilization happens, not how.

Nature Description Categories
Admin-fulfilled No on-site utilization needed. Rewards admin processes via Ops Dashboard after service is delivered. Hotel Business & Travel – Points Exchange Gift voucher – hotel (for others → gift certificate)
On-site utilization Guest presents or staff identifies the voucher at the outlet. Used as payment method or entitlement at point of consumption. Dining Wellness & Recreation Gift voucher – restaurant Retail collection Gift voucher – hotel (self, as payment) Business & Travel – Others

Utilization Methods (Revised)

The revised proposal consolidates the initial 5 methods into 2 primary methods + 1 monitored fallback. The change is specific: we eliminate the 2 guest-initiated outlet QR scanning flows (initial methods 4–5) and replace them with a staff web tool. We also formalize the room lookup flow for staying guests (initially underspecified as method 1). Methods 2–3 from the initial design (guest shows QR, staff scans it) carry forward largely unchanged.

Section 2.4

What Actually Changes vs What Stays the Same

The revision is targeted, not a wholesale redesign. This section is deliberately explicit about the scope of changes to avoid overselling the simplification.

What changes (3 things)

1. Guest-initiated outlet QR flows are eliminated
Initial methods 4 and 5 — where the guest scans an outlet QR code, navigates to a website/landing page, selects their voucher, and shows confirmation to staff — are removed. Instead, staff at non-Field-App outlets use a new staff web tool to scan the guest's QR or enter a code. This means physical outlet QR codes and the Utilization Landing Page are no longer needed.
2. Room/name lookup is clarified for staying guests
Initial method 1 ("staff via Field App, no QR") was underspecified — it didn't define how staff identifies the guest. The revised documentation makes this explicit: staff looks up the guest by room number or name via PMS integration in the Field App. The method itself is the same; we're just documenting the steps. This still requires Rewards voucher data to be integrated into the Field App (currently not available).
3. A staff web tool is introduced
A new lightweight browser-based tool for outlets without Field App. This is what replaces the guest-initiated outlet QR flows — staff scans the guest's QR or enters a code, instead of the guest driving the process. This is a net-new build.

What stays the same

  • Guests still show a QR on their phone — for non-staying guests (Method 2), this is unchanged from initial methods 2–3. The guest opens their voucher QR from their Rewards account or redemption email, and staff scans it.
  • Self vs others distinction still exists. Members show QR from their account; recipients show QR from their email. The source of the QR differs. The staff process happens to be the same for both, but the system still distinguishes them.
  • Staying vs non-staying decision still exists. Staff still needs to determine whether the guest is a staying guest (eligible for room lookup) or not. This branching factor is unchanged.
  • Staff still needs training on which method applies. The decision tree is simpler (staying → room lookup, otherwise → QR scan, fallback → code entry) but staff still must assess the situation and choose the right path.
  • The voucher category classification is unchanged. The same 8 categories map to the same 2 natures (admin-fulfilled vs on-site).
  • Admin-fulfilled vouchers are unchanged. Hotel and Business & Travel – Points Exchange vouchers are still processed via Ops Dashboard.
  • Anti-double-utilization is unchanged. Still fully system-managed at the backend level.
  • Most infrastructure remains. Field App, Rewards website QR display, redemption email QR — all still needed. We add a staff web tool and remove outlet QR codes + Utilization Landing Page.
*
Net assessment: The revision eliminates 2 guest-initiated multi-step flows and replaces them with staff-initiated equivalents. It formalizes one underspecified method. Everything else — the branching logic, the guest actions for QR-based flows, the category system, the fraud model — carries forward from the initial design.
Section 2.5

Fraud Prevention & Compliance

The revised flow maintains compliance by assigning each primary utilization method its own proof of entitlement. For the two primary methods, knowing the voucher code alone is not sufficient to utilize a voucher. The fallback method (code entry) is an exception — it relies on operational controls (monitoring, restricted access) rather than technical prevention. This fraud model is essentially unchanged from the initial design — the same threats apply, and the same mitigations are in place.

Threat Analysis

!
Threat: Code sharing

Member shares voucher code with a friend. Both go to different outlets simultaneously.

Method 1: Cannot share a room check-in. PMS verifies the guest is actually in that room.
Method 2: Friend cannot generate the secure token without login access to the member's account or the recipient's email.
Method 3 (fallback): Vulnerable — code can be shared. Mitigated by monitoring and restricted staff access.
All methods: Backend prevents double utilization regardless.
!
Threat: Staff collusion

Rogue staff sees voucher list, shares codes with accomplices who visit the outlet.

Method 1: Staff sees voucher details, but only for verified in-house guests via PMS. Cannot browse arbitrary member vouchers. Staff could share the code, but knowing the code alone is not enough to utilize the voucher (the accomplice would still need a valid secure token or room lookup).
Method 2: Staff may see the voucher code on the details screen after scanning the QR. However, the code alone is insufficient — utilization requires the secure token from the QR. A shared code cannot be used through normal methods.
Method 3 (fallback): Most vulnerable — staff sees the code and the code is sufficient for utilization via this method. Mitigated by restricting fallback access to limited/senior staff and monitoring usage patterns.
~
Accepted risk: Room impersonation

Non-guest approaches staff and claims a room number to use a staying guest's voucher.

Accepted for guest convenience at a luxury hotel. Mitigations: staff training to verify guest identity, audit logging of all lookups, optional photo match if PMS stores guest photos.
~
Accepted risk: Staff browsing rooms

Staff tries different room numbers on Field App to discover available vouchers.

Accepted as a trade-off for the in-house guest flow. Mitigations: audit trail of all searches, anomaly detection on unusual patterns, restrict searches to currently assigned outlets/areas.

Anti-Double-Utilization

Double utilization prevention remains fully system-managed at the backend level. Regardless of which method is used, the system marks the voucher as utilized upon staff confirmation. Any subsequent attempt — at any outlet, by any method — is rejected immediately. This is unchanged from the current system.

QR Token Approach (Open for Decision)

The QR code must contain more than the plain voucher code to prevent code-sharing fraud. Several approaches are viable:

Option A: Signed JWT (JSON Web Token)
QR encodes a short-lived token signed with a server-side secret. Contains voucher ID + member/recipient ID + expiry timestamp. Cannot be forged. Can include a short TTL (e.g., 5–10 minutes) to prevent screenshot sharing. This is the most standard approach for token-based verification.
Option B: HMAC-signed code
Simpler variant: voucher code + a hash (HMAC) that proves it was generated by the server. Lighter than JWT, same core principle — the code alone is insufficient without the server-generated signature.
Option C: One-time dynamic code
System generates a fresh, short-lived code each time the member opens the QR screen. Expires in minutes. Strongest protection against screenshots and sharing, but requires the member to be online when showing the QR.
💡
Recommendation: The specific token approach should be decided during technical design. The key requirement is that the QR encodes something that cannot be reproduced by knowing only the voucher code, and ideally has a short expiry window to limit screenshot sharing.
Section 2.6

Staff Web Tool

To avoid the guest-initiated outlet QR flows proposed in the initial design, a lightweight staff-facing web tool is introduced for outlets without the Field App. This is a net-new tool — it enables staff at these outlets to scan voucher QR codes and confirm utilization via a browser, without requiring a native app install.

What it is
A web-based tool (no app install) that lets outlet staff scan voucher QR codes and confirm utilization. Accessed via smartphone browser.
QR scanning via browser
Uses browser camera API with JavaScript-based decoding. Works on Safari 11+ (iOS 11+) and Chrome on Android 6.0+. HTTPS required. Slightly slower than native app (1–2 seconds extra).
Security
Staff authenticates with credentials scoped to their outlet. Session management via secure tokens. Only utilization actions permitted — no admin functions.
Development scope
Single-page web app: login, scan/search, voucher details, confirm button. Calls same backend APIs as Field App. Estimated a few weeks of development.
✓
Compatibility: Any smartphone from 2016+ with a modern browser (Chrome or Safari). The main variable is autofocus quality — very cheap phones may be slightly slower to lock onto QR codes. For staff devices at AYANA outlets, this should not be an issue.
Section 2.7

Open Items & Dependencies

AYANA Rewards voucher integration to Field App
The Field App already has PMS integration (room search, guest name, membership tier). What's missing is AYANA Rewards voucher data — staff needs to see a guest's available vouchers when looking up a room. This is the primary dependency for Method 1 (room lookup).
Blocker for Method 1
Staff web tool development
Required for non-Field-App outlets to support Method 2 (QR scan) without guest-initiated workarounds. This is a new build — lightweight single-page web app with QR scanning capability.
Required for full rollout
Audit logging scope
Utilization events (who utilized which voucher, when, via which method) should be logged — this is standard and likely already planned. The open question is whether to also log every voucher view/lookup that doesn't result in utilization. Comprehensive logging provides a paper trail for investigating fraud, but generates a large volume of data that may never be reviewed. Consider logging utilization events as a baseline, and only adding view-level logging if fraud patterns emerge or if audit requirements demand it.
Design decision
QR token technical design
The specific token approach for the QR code (JWT, HMAC, or dynamic code) needs to be decided during technical design. See Section 5.3 for options.
Technical design decision
Transition period
Until Rewards voucher integration is live on the Field App, all properties default to Method 2 (QR scan) for all guests, including staying guests. The in-house guest flow (Method 1) becomes available per-property as the integration rolls out. This means the current manual code lookup process continues as-is during the transition.
