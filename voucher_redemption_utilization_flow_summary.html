Part 1

Voucher Redemption

This section covers what information the member provides when redeeming a voucher — the "for me or others" choice and the additional info form per category.

1.1 For Me or For Others

The member selects whether the voucher is for themselves or someone else. This choice is locked at redemption — changing it after would invalidate any redemption email already sent to the recipient.

Field Rule
Self / Others Mandatory. Locked once submitted — cannot be switched after redemption.
Receiver name Mandatory if "for others." Locked once submitted.
Receiver email Optional. Can only be filled at redemption — no fill-later. If provided, triggers a redemption email with the voucher to the recipient. Locked once submitted.
i
Business & Travel – Points Exchange: No separate self/others step. The member enters the external account ID to receive the points (e.g. Krisflyer member number). This could be their own account or someone else's — the flow is the same either way, since the member just inputs the target account ID in the form field.

1.2 Additional Info

All categories have a Notes field with dynamic placeholder text tailored to the category (e.g. preferred dates for hotels, dietary preferences for dining). This is frontend-only — the form structure stays the same, only the placeholder string changes.

On-site utilization vouchers (Dining, Wellness & Rec, Gift – restaurant, Retail collection, B&T – Others)

Optional notes at redemption only — no fill-later. Placeholder text varies by category (e.g. Dining: "e.g. Any dietary requirements or preferences"; Retail: "e.g. Preferred pickup date or location").

Category Fields When
Dining Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only
Wellness & Recreation Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only
Gift voucher – restaurant Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only
Retail collection Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only
Business & Travel – Others Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only

Hotel vouchers (Hotel, Gift voucher – hotel)

Single Notes field with dynamic placeholder (e.g. "e.g. Preferred stay date, number of rooms, or any special requests"). No structured date/room fields — those details are handled during the booking/fulfillment process.

Category Fields When
Hotel Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only
Gift voucher – hotel Notes (optional, dynamic placeholder) At redemption only

Business & Travel – Points Exchange

Requires Account ID, Name, and optional Notes at redemption (no fill-later). No self/others choice. The form shows dynamic helper text per product — e.g. Flow Coins: "Don't have a Flow account? No worries — enter your email and we'll set it up for you." Krisflyer shows standard labels only.

Category Fields When Note
B&T – Points Exchange Account ID Name Notes (optional) Required at redemption Dynamic helper text per product

1.3 Post-Redemption CTA

The voucher detail page shows a CTA that opens a prefilled email to the Rewards team — visible to both the member and the recipient (via the redemption email link).

Hotel / Gift voucher – hotel
CTA label: "Ready to book? Contact us"
These categories need reservation coordination (dates, rooms, special requests). The CTA opens a prefilled email to the reservations/Rewards inbox.
Dining / Wellness / Gift – restaurant / Retail / B&T – Others
CTA label: "Questions about this voucher? Contact us"
Lighter wording — these are typically walk-in/on-site vouchers. The CTA serves as a general support channel.
Email field Content
To Dedicated Rewards inbox (e.g. rewards@ayana.com)
Subject Voucher Inquiry — [Voucher Code] [Voucher Name]
Body Prefilled with: member name, membership tier, voucher type, quantity, voucher code. For "for others" recipients, the body identifies them as the recipient and includes the gifter's name.
i
Who sees it: For "for me" vouchers, the member sees the CTA on their voucher detail page. For "for others" vouchers, both the member (in their redeemed voucher list) and the recipient (on the voucher page from the redemption email) see the CTA — with slightly different prefilled email body text so the Rewards team knows who they're speaking with.
*
Exception — B&T – Points Exchange: No CTA. Points Exchange vouchers are processed automatically once the account details are submitted at redemption. There is nothing to coordinate after.

Part 2

Voucher Utilization

Utilization is the process of using a redeemed voucher at an outlet or having it fulfilled by the Rewards admin.

Section 2.1

How Voucher Utilization Works Today

Currently, the member shows their voucher code and staff searches for it in the Rewards system to mark it as used. No QR scanning, no Field App integration. This works but has limitations: the code is exposed in plain text (fraud risk), there's no identity verification, and it relies on manual staff lookup.

Section 2.2

Proposed Utilization Flow

The proposed flow uses 2 primary methods + 1 monitored fallback, all staff-initiated. The method depends on: outlet capability (Field App availability), user type (self vs others), and guest status (staying vs non-staying).

Voucher Nature Classification

Admin-fulfilled vouchers are handled via Ops Dashboard; on-site vouchers require staff action at the outlet.

Nature Description Categories
Admin-fulfilled No on-site utilization needed. Rewards admin processes via Ops Dashboard after service is delivered. Hotel Business & Travel – Points Exchange Gift voucher – hotel (for others → gift certificate)
On-site utilization Guest presents or staff identifies the voucher at the outlet. Used as payment method or entitlement at point of consumption. Dining Wellness & Recreation Gift voucher – restaurant Retail collection Gift voucher – hotel (self, as payment) Business & Travel – Others

Utilization Methods

Decision tree: staying guest + Field App → room lookup (Method 1), otherwise → QR scan (Method 2), QR not possible → manual code entry (Method 3, fallback).

Method 1  ·  In-house Guest Flow
Room / Name Lookup
For staying guests at properties with Field App. Guest does not need their phone.
1
Guest arrives at outlet
Identifies themselves verbally or staff recognizes them.
2
Staff searches by room number or name
Using the Field App. System cross-checks with PMS to verify the guest is currently checked in.
3
Field App shows guest's available vouchers
Only vouchers valid for that outlet are displayed.
4
Staff selects voucher, guest confirms, done
One tap to mark as utilized. System records the transaction.
Requirements
Field App with AYANA Rewards voucher integration + guest data from PMS
Applicable to
Staying guests (self-use) at properties with Field App
Identity verification
PMS confirms room occupant. Hotel check-in already verified guest ID.
Anti-fraud
Staff can see voucher details after lookup, but access is scoped to verified in-house guests only — staff cannot browse arbitrary member vouchers. The primary control is PMS verification, not code secrecy.
fallback when method 1 is not available
Method 2  ·  Standard Flow
QR Scan
Universal method for all other scenarios. Covers non-staying guests, recipients (for others), and properties without PMS integration.
1
Guest opens their voucher QR
Members: from their Rewards account (requires login).
Recipients (for others): from the redemption email link (unique per recipient, dynamically generated).
2
Staff scans the QR
Using the Field App or the staff web tool (for non-Field-App outlets). The QR contains a secure token — not the plain voucher code.
3
System validates and shows voucher details
Decrypts/verifies the token, checks voucher is valid and not yet used, displays details to staff.
4
Staff confirms utilization
One tap. System marks voucher as used. Done.
Requirements
Field App or staff web tool with camera access. HTTPS required.
Applicable to
All guests at all properties — non-staying guests, recipients, and staying guests where Rewards voucher integration is not yet live.
Identity verification
Secure token in QR proves access to member account or recipient email. Cannot be generated without login/email access.
Anti-fraud
The QR contains a secure token that cannot be generated from the voucher code alone — knowing or sharing the code is not enough to utilize the voucher. Staff may see the voucher code on the details screen, but it is not useful without the token.
fallback when method 2 is not possible
Method 3  ·  Fallback
Manual Code Entry
For edge cases where QR scanning is not possible (camera not working, guest unable to show voucher on phone). Staff enters the voucher code manually.
1
Guest provides voucher code
Guest reads the code aloud from their phone/email, or shows the code on screen without QR.
2
Staff enters code into Field App or staff web tool
System looks up the voucher, displays details to staff.
3
Staff confirms utilization
System marks voucher as used.
*
Fraud note: Weakest method since the plain voucher code is exposed. Mitigations: (1) monitor all utilizations via this method, (2) restrict access to senior staff roles. Second-factor verification (e.g. OTP) can be added later if needed.
